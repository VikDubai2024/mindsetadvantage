Stino Freestyle

    Stijn discovered freestyle tennis literally on the tennis court, when as a 13-year-old he was challenged by a trainer to juggle on the frame of his racket. He was immediately triggered by the challenge and tried to improve his personal best every week. Until one day... he achieved a higher number (more balls) than the trainer. When Stijn saw Stefan Bojic (a Serbian who is considered one of the pioneers of contemporary Freestyle Tennis) working on Instagram in the same period, it inspired him to further develop his passion for freestyling. In 2021, COVID-19 and the corona crisis struck mercilessly and Stijn was also forced to stay 'in his room'. Although the world was shut down for months, Stijn saw the unique opportunity in the crisis to take his tennis trick shots to the next level . He spent days, weeks and months in his garden, teaching himself new and more advanced tricks. When he posted some videos on social media as an experiment and participated in the #TennisLivesOn challenge of the Tennisplaza news site, he received an unexpected number of positive reactions. Stijn's passion, in combination with hard work and drive, resulted a year later in his first 'booking' at a fair in Waregem and a widely appreciated act at the European Open in Antwerp, the largest tennis tournament in Belgium. In 2020, Stijn took part in a freestyle competition at the European Open. He came in second. In 2021, he was challenged by his great idol Stefan Bojic during an improvisation round to perform some tricks on the center court of the Lotto Arena.
